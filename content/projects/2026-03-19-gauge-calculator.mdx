## what is it

if you knit or crochet, you know the drill: you make a swatch, count your stitches and rows, measure the width, and then do math to figure out how many stitches you actually need for your project. this tool does that last part for you.

you enter your swatch measurements — stitches, rows, and width — and it calculates your stitches per inch (SPI) and rows per inch (RPI). then you can plug in your desired project width and it tells you exactly how many stitches to cast on.

it even handles pattern repeats. if your stitch pattern repeats every 4 stitches (like a k2, p2 rib or a crochet shell repeat), it'll adjust your stitch count so the repeat fits evenly. you can add an offset for edge stitches or turning chains too.

## how it works

it's a [sinatra](https://sinatrarb.com/) app backed by two gems i wrote:

- **fiber_units** — a unit conversion library for fiber craft measurements (inches, centimeters, stitches, rows)
- **fiber_gauge** — the gauge math itself: takes your swatch data and calculates SPI, RPI, and required stitches/rows for a target dimension

the frontend is a simple erb form that hits a JSON API. no javascript framework, no build step. you click a button, it does a fetch, and the results show up.

## what is it

fiber_gauge is a ruby gem that takes your swatch measurements and does the gauge math for you. you tell it how many stitches and rows you counted over a measured width, and it gives you stitches per inch (SPI), rows per inch (RPI), and can calculate how many stitches you need to hit a target width.

## what it does

- **SPI and RPI** — the two numbers every knitter and crocheter needs from their swatch
- **required stitches** — "i want this piece to be 14 inches wide, how many stitches do i cast on?" (or chain, if you're crocheting)
- **width for stitches** — the reverse: "i have 80 stitches, how wide will that be?"
- built on [fiber_units](/projects/fiber-units) so all inputs and outputs are typed measurements

## why

gauge math is simple division, but i do it often enough and get it wrong often enough that it deserved its own gem. works the same whether you're knitting or crocheting — stitches are stitches. it also powers the [gauge calculator](/projects/gauge-calculator) web app.

## example

```ruby
# knitting swatch
knit_gauge = FiberGauge::Gauge.new(
  stitches: 20.stitches,
  rows: 28.rows,
  width: 4.inches
)

knit_gauge.spi                          # => 5.0
knit_gauge.required_stitches(14.inches) # => 70.stitches

# crochet swatch — same API, different numbers
crochet_gauge = FiberGauge::Gauge.new(
  stitches: 14.stitches,
  rows: 16.rows,
  width: 4.inches
)

crochet_gauge.spi                          # => 3.5
crochet_gauge.required_stitches(12.inches) # => 42.stitches
```
